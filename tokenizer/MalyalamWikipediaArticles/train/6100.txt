In some cases these are distinguished by specifying the number of atoms as in carbon monoxide and carbon dioxide, and in other cases by specifying the element's oxidation number, as in iron oxide and iron oxide. Certain elements can form many different oxides, such as those of nitrogen.
Carbon dioxide is the main product of fossil fuel combustion.
Carbon monoxide is the product of the incomplete combustion of carbon-based fuels and precursor to many useful chemicals.
Nitrogen dioxide is a problematic pollutant from internal combustion engines.
Sulfur dioxide, the principal oxide of sulfur, is emitted from volcanoes.
Nitrous oxide is a potent greenhouse gas produced by soil bacteria.
